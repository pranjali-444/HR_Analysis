## 📌 Project Overview
- This project presents an end-to-end HR attrition analysis where key HR business questions were answered using **SQL** and the insights were validated and communicated through **Power BI dashboards**.  
- The analysis focuses on **why employees are leaving the company**, leveraging data-driven storytelling to help stakeholders understand attrition patterns and contributing factors.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze overall employee attrition trends  
- Identify departments and job roles with high attrition  
- Understand the impact of salary, age, tenure, and job satisfaction on attrition  
- Examine early-career attrition patterns  
- Build an interactive dashboard to narrate the attrition story for decision-makers  

---

## 🗂️ Dataset Description
The analysis is based on an HR employee dataset containing the following key attributes:

- **Employee Demographics** – Age, gender, marital status, education, education field  
- **Job Details** – Department, job role, job level, business travel, overtime  
- **Compensation** – Monthly income, salary slab, percent salary hike, stock options  
- **Satisfaction & Engagement** – Job satisfaction, environment satisfaction, work-life balance  
- **Tenure Metrics** – Years at company, years in current role, years since last promotion  
- **Attrition Indicator** – Whether the employee left the organization

## 📈 Power BI Dashboard Highlights
- KPI cards for total employees, attrition count, and attrition rate  
- Bar charts showing attrition by job role, department, and salary slab  
- Line and column charts for tenure-based attrition trends  
- Donut and stacked visuals for demographic analysis  
- Interactive slicers for department, age group, and salary filtering  
- A story-driven layout to guide stakeholders through key attrition insights  

---

## 📌 Key Insights
- Attrition is significantly higher among employees in lower salary slabs  
- Early-tenure employees are more likely to leave the organization  
- Certain job roles and departments contribute disproportionately to attrition  
- Overtime and low job satisfaction are strong indicators of attrition risk

## 🚀 Business Impact

- Identify high-risk attrition segments

- Improve compensation and engagement policies  
- Reduce early-career employee turnover
